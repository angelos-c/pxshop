# Project X Tuning — Brand Brief

For feeding into copy generators / AI writing tools. Keep any generated copy consistent with this.

## What we are

A curated automotive parts e-shop — not a warehouse of everything, a short, deliberate list of
brands we've chosen to stock (Eventuri, Black Mamba, ZRP, CTS Turbo, Maxton Design, Milltek,
Mishimoto). The pitch is editorial and confident, closer to a design magazine or a fashion
e-commerce site (OUTFIT is the visual reference point) than a typical parts catalogue. We sell
fewer things, chosen properly.

## Visual identity

- **Two colours only.** Brand blue (`#0c28ff`) and off-white/cream (`#ffffff`). No third colour,
  no gradients, no tints beyond opacity of the same blue. If a new UI element needs a colour,
  it's one of these two, inverted.
- **Zero border radius everywhere.** Square corners on every button, card, input, image. This is
  a strict rule, not a default — never round anything.
- **Hairline rules, not boxes.** Sections are separated by thin (or occasionally bold, 3–5px)
  horizontal rules, not cards, shadows, or containers. No drop shadows, no card chrome.
- **Type does the heavy lifting.** Neue Haas Grotesk Text Pro (bold/black weights), huge and
  tight-tracked for headlines (`PROJECT [X].`), small and uppercase with wide tracking for labels
  and nav (`BAG (00)`, `SEE ALL EVENTURI`). Contrast between "shout" and "whisper" type is the
  main visual device — there's almost no in-between size.
- **Text is the CTA.** Links and buttons are bold uppercase text with a hover fade, not filled
  buttons with borders/backgrounds. "SEE ALL X →" not a button labelled "Shop Now".
- **Editorial grids.** Products sit in plain grids or horizontal-scroll rows, framed by rules —
  never in shadowed cards.

## Voice & tone

- **Confident, terse, declarative.** Short sentences. No exclamation points, no hedging, no
  "amazing deals" retail-speak.
- **Precise over hypey.** State the fact plainly and let the product/brand carry it —
  "Genuine Eventuri part, officially imported by Project X Tuning" rather than adjectives.
- **A little dry wit is fine in headline moments** — e.g. the footer line "Built to be judged."
  This is the exception, not the rule; most copy should be plain and functional.
- **Curation is the core claim.** Repeated language: "curated", "carefully selected", "officially
  authorised and licensed dealer". We're gatekeepers, not a marketplace — never imply "wide
  selection" or "something for everyone".
- **No emoji, no slang, no urgency gimmicks** ("HURRY!", "LIMITED TIME"). If something is
  genuinely time-boxed, state it flatly (see the announcement ticker copy).

## Reference copy already in the product

- Hero: `PROJECT [X].`
- Footer statement: `Built to be judged.`
- Footer tagline: `Officially authorised and licensed dealer for carefully curated automotive
  brand collections.`
- Nav/labels: `BAG (00)`, `ALL PRODUCTS`, `SEE ALL EVENTURI →`
- Announcement ticker: `Now available: same-day in-house permanent unlocks for all BMW 2020+
  (inc. G80, G81, G82, X6M...and more!)` / `All in stock parts available in 1-2 days at our
  Limassol location.`

## Quick checklist for new copy

1. Uppercase for labels/nav, sentence case for body copy.
2. Short. If a sentence needs a comma to justify itself, split it.
3. Say what it is, not how great it is.
4. No rounded-corner or drop-shadow language implied in UI descriptions either.
5. When in doubt, cut it down further.
